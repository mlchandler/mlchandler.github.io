Mitchell Chandler

mlchandler@ucsd.edu

school

I am a third-year physical oceanography graduate student at Scripps Institution of Oceanography advised by Nathalie Zilberman and Janet Sprintall, and am the recipient of a Fulbright student fellowship.

My thesis work aims to combine observations from multiple observing systems to produce estimates of velocity between the ocean surface and 1975-m depth over longer time periods than most other subsurface velocity time series. These estimates are used to examine trends and variability in western boundary currents, which are major ocean currents found at the western boundaries of ocean basins. Part of my work also aims to examine relationships between western boundary current variability and the occurrence of marine heatwaves (periods of anomalously warm water). These heatwave events can have devastating consequences on weather, fisheries, and marine ecosystems.

Originally from New Zealand, I completed my BSc at the University of Otago in 2017 and did a further Honours year at the University of Auckland in 2018. For my BSc(Hons) I used satellite altimetry to examine trends and variability in the surface expression of the Fiordland Current, which is a poleward-flowing boundary current off of south-west New Zealand. My advisors for this work were Melissa Bowen (Auckland) and Robert Smith (Otago).

Twitter Posters

Twitter posters were brought to my attention by Mike Morrison and were conceived as an alternative to conferences cancelled as a result of the covid-19 pandemic. These Twitter Posters are intended to be a relatively low effort (after all you have already done the hard work of conducting your research!) way of communicating science to a wider group of people using GIFs to present a series of simple slides on twitter. For more information about Twitter Posters see Mike's youtube video (LINK) where he also provides templates to create your own!
