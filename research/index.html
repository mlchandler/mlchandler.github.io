Research

Argo and HR-XBT Observing Networks

This project aims to combine complementary Argo and HR-XBT datasets to improve long-term estimates of mass and heat transport in both the ocean's interior and boundary currents and is based on the work by Zilberman et al. 2018.

A poster on this work was presented at Ocean Sciences Meeting 2020 in San Diego, and a pdf of the poster can be viewed here.

This work is ongoing and is advised by Janet Sprintall and Nathalie Zilberman.

Fiordland Current

The Fiordland Current is a poleward-flowing EBC along the southwest coast of New Zealand. Satellite altimetry was used to estimate the mean, interannual variability, decadal variability, and longer-term trends in the across-track surface geostrophic velocity of the current. Possible drivers of trends and variability were identified. A paper has been published on this work.

This work was advised by Melissa Bowen and Robert Smith.
