Research

Argo and HR-XBT Observing Networks

This project builds on work by Zilberman et al. 2018 and aims to combine complementary Argo and HR-XBT datasets to improve long-term estimates of mass and heat transport in boundary current regions and in the ocean's interior.

A poster on this work was presented at Ocean Sciences Meeting 2020 (pdf).

This work is ongoing and is advised by Janet Sprintall and Nathalie Zilberman.

Fiordland Current

The Fiordland Current is a poleward-flowing eastern boundary current (EBC) along the southwest coast of New Zealand. Satellite altimetry was used to estimate the mean, interannual variability, decadal variability, and longer-term trends in the across-track surface geostrophic velocity of the current. Variability and trends in the current were related to possible drivers.

A paper has been published on this work.

This study was the result of my BSc(Hons) which was advised by Melissa Bowen and Robert Smith.
